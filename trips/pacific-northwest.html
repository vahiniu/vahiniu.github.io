🚐 Pacific Northwest Parks Road Trip

📍 California, Oregon & Washington, USA ⏱️ 9 days

A campervan loop from San Francisco through four national parks — Redwoods, Crater Lake, Mt. Rainier, and Olympic — ending with a night in Portland before the long drive home.

🗺️ Itinerary
Day 1

SF to Redwoods

Pick up the van and drive ~340 miles north. Evening hike (Lady Bird Johnson Grove or Trillium Falls). Camp at Mill Creek Campground.

Day 2

Redwoods to Crater Lake

Morning hike (Fern Canyon or Boy Scout Tree Trail) before driving ~214 miles toward Crater Lake.

Day 3

Crater Lake to Mt. Rainier

Sunrise hike up Garfield Peak, then a long drive ~387 miles north.

Day 4

Mt. Rainier

Hike the Paradise region — Skyline Loop, Golden Gate Trail, or Comet Falls.

Day 5

Rainier to Olympic

Drive ~117 miles to Olympic NP. Camp at Sol Duc Hot Springs; campfire and s'mores.

Day 6

Olympic National Park

Hikes near Sol Duc — Marymere Falls, Sol Duc Falls.

Day 7

Coast & rainforest

Ozette Triangle Loop or the Hoh Rainforest Hall of Mosses, Mt. Storm King, or Shi Shi Beach.

Day 8

Olympic to Portland

Drive ~162 miles to Portland; chase waterfalls and a night out in the city.

Day 9

Portland to SF

Long ~636-mile drive back home.

📌 Things to do
  • Redwoods NP — Avenue of the Giants, James Irvine Trail, Fern Canyon
  • Crater Lake NP — Garfield Peak sunrise hike, Watchman Peak, Discovery Point
  • Mt. Rainier NP — Skyline Loop, Golden Gate Trail, Comet Falls
  • Olympic NP — Marymere & Sol Duc Falls, Hoh Rainforest, Mt. Storm King, Shi Shi Beach
  • Sol Duc Hot Springs soak
  • Waterfall hunting around Portland
🍴 Where to eat
  • Crater Lake Lodge — breakfast or lunch with a view
🛏️ Where to stay
  • Mill Creek Campground, Del Norte Coast Redwood SP
  • Waterwheel RV Park, Chiloquin, OR (Crater Lake)
  • Elkamp Eastcreek, Mineral, WA (Mt. Rainier)
  • Sol Duc Hot Springs Resort RV sites (Olympic)
  • Lake Quinault, Willaby Campground (Olympic)
💡 Good to know
  • A campervan gets ~13-15 mpg; budget for frequent fill-ups on the long legs.
  • Some trails (e.g. Tall Trees Grove) need permits booked ahead.
  • Shi Shi Beach requires a Makah Recreation Pass.
  • Check road closures in advance.
