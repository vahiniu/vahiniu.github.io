🏔️ Peru

📍 Cusco, Salkantay & Lima, Peru ⏱️ 14 days

A two-week Peru trip combining Cusco and the Sacred Valley, the multi-day Salkantay trek to Machu Picchu, and time in Lima's food and coastal scene.

🗺️ Itinerary
Days 1-2

Arrive via Lima to Cusco

Fly into Lima (grab a SIM and soles), then on to Cusco. Walk around town and book Sacred Valley tours.

Day 3

Cusco — festival & food

Catch the Inti Raymi festival and do a food tour around Cusco.

Day 4

Rainbow Mountain

Day tour to Rainbow Mountain (Vinicunca).

Day 5

Sacred Valley

Moray, the Maras salt mines, Chinchero, Pisac, and Ollantaytambo.

Day 6

Salkantay Trek begins

Drive to Soraypampa, hike up to Humantay Lake, camp at Quiswarniyoc.

Day 7

Salkantay Pass

Early start over the pass, then a long descent. Camp at Chaullay.

Day 8

Jungle & hot springs

Hike to La Playa Sahuayaco and soak in the Santa Teresa hot springs.

Day 9

Llactapata to Aguas Calientes

Hike past the Llactapata ruins, ending at a hotel in Aguas Calientes.

Day 10

Machu Picchu

Pre-dawn start (option to add Huayna Picchu), then evening train back to Cusco.

Days 11-14

Lima

Fly to Lima, stay in Barranco. Optional Huacachina day trip, a walking tour, and dining before flying home.

📌 Things to do
  • Salkantay Trek to Machu Picchu — multi-day high-altitude trek
  • Machu Picchu — with optional Huayna Picchu add-on
  • Inti Raymi festival in Cusco (late June)
  • Rainbow Mountain (Vinicunca) day tour
  • Sacred Valley — Moray, Maras salt mines, Chinchero, Pisac, Ollantaytambo
  • Humantay Lake & Santa Teresa hot springs
  • Cusco food tour
  • Huacachina desert oasis day trip from Lima
🍴 Where to eat
  • The Bistro 390 (Cusco) — American-style, great risotto
  • El Trio & Ceviche Calle (Cusco) — ceviche
  • Astrid y Gastón (Lima) — acclaimed fine dining
🛏️ Where to stay
  • Hostel in central Cusco
  • Hostel in the Barranco district, Lima
  • Camps along the Salkantay trek (Quiswarniyoc, Chaullay, Santa Teresa)
  • Hotel in Aguas Calientes — base for Machu Picchu
💡 Good to know
  • Buy a SIM and exchange soles in central Lima for better rates.
  • Acclimatize in Cusco before the trek; nights on the trail drop below freezing.
  • Bring small soles in cash for trail fees and showers.
  • Book Machu Picchu and trek reservations well in advance.
