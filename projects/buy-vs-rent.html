The percentage view — rent yield vs cost-of-owning bandiRent yield = one year of rent ÷ home value. The blue band = what owning costs per year as a % of home value, from day 1 (mostly financed with the bank’s cheap money) to paid-off (all your own money, which could be earning elsewhere). Where the green rent line sits against the band is the whole decision.

Fixed background assumptions: property tax grows 2%/yr (Prop 13-style), other ownership and rental figures use the growth rates you set, portfolio taxed at the cap-gains slider rate on gains at the horizon, ADU/rental income modeled at an effective 15% tax after expenses and depreciation. USD mode applies the $750k federal / $1M California interest-deduction limits against the live loan balance and a $500k home-sale gain exclusion. This is a model, not advice — the two sliders that matter most are appreciation and investment return.
