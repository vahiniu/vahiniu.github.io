Radar image denoising with autoencoders

IEEE Radar Conference (RadarConf), 2018 · with S. Vishwakarma, A. Iqbal, A. Majumdar & S. S. Ram (IIIT-Delhi)

Radar can see through walls — but the wall wrecks the picture. We taught a neural network to fix it.

As a radar signal passes through a wall, attenuation, refraction, and multipath reflections distort the returned image, so a person or object imaged through a wall looks very different from the same target in open line-of-sight. Classical corrections need to know the wall's thickness and material and the room geometry — information you rarely have in practice.

In this work we treated the distortion as a denoising problem. We trained a denoising autoencoder — a neural network that learns to map a corrupted input back to a clean version — on pairs of radar images: the distorted through-wall image as input, and the corresponding clean line-of-sight image as the target. After training on a large set of such pairs, the network learns to undo the wall's effects and reconstruct an image close to the free-space one, with no prior knowledge of the wall parameters or room layout.

The paper, "Mitigation of through-wall interference in radar images using denoising autoencoders," was published at the IEEE Radar Conference (RadarConf) in 2018.
