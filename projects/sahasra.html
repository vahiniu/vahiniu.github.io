Sahasra.AI

Digital health startup · co-founder & CEO · IIIT-Delhi

A mental-health-first digital health startup I co-founded and led — building one life-course record of how you're doing, body and mind.

A walkthrough of the app

Sahasra.AI was an early-stage digital health startup I co-founded and ran as CEO, incubated at IIIT-Delhi. The idea: a mental-health-first, proactive wellness platform that gives people a single, life-course record of how they're doing — physically and mentally — and uses it to nudge them toward better outcomes, rather than waiting until something goes wrong.

The product bet was interoperability: health data only becomes useful when it can move. We built around open health-data standards (HL7, SNOMED CT, ICD-10) so records aren't siloed, and around India's national digital-health rails so a person's record could follow them across providers. That national layer is ABDM — the Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission, India's government-backed digital health infrastructure. ABDM gives every citizen a unique health account (an ABHA ID) and shared standards for securely linking and sharing records across hospitals, labs, and apps, always with the individual's consent. Integrating with ABDM meant a user's data was portable and interoperable by design instead of trapped in one more app.

On top of that record we layered AI to make care proactive. For physical health, algorithms assembled a longitudinal, holistic profile to surface trends and risks across a person's timeline. For mental health, a digital journal and mood tracker fed a transformer-based emotion model organized around Plutchik's wheel of emotions — the eight primary emotions (joy, trust, fear, surprise, sadness, disgust, anger, anticipation) and their blends. This "Plutchik transformer" read a user's journal and chat text and mapped it onto that emotional spectrum, turning free-form writing into a structured, trackable signal of how someone was really feeling over time.

As a founder, though, my job went well beyond the product. I owned the market strategy — defining who Sahasra was for (individuals, employers with a workforce to keep healthy, large institutions like universities, and downstream partners in pharma, insurance, research, and government) and how we would reach and retain them. I built the business and financial model — a subscription plus pay-as-you-go mix with employer-focused plans and premium services — along with the revenue projections, key metrics, and a phased roadmap behind it. And I put the whole story into a pitch: I authored the deck, framed the problem and market, and pitched Sahasra to our incubator and prospective partners to raise support and resources.
